# Reference Projects

- **QBE**
  - URLs: https://c9x.me/compile/ , https://c9x.me/compile/doc/il.html , https://c9x.me/compile/doc/abi.html
  - Authority tier: high-value companion
  - Required or optional: recommended
  - Consult when: you want a compact backend and ABI reference with a small, readable IR
  - Related chapters: 9, 13, 18, 19, 20
  - Why it matters: QBE is one of the best “small but serious” backends to study

- **chibicc**
  - URL: https://github.com/rui314/chibicc
  - Authority tier: high-value companion
  - Required or optional: recommended
  - Consult when: you want to see a readable end-to-end C compiler with clear tokenizer/parser/backend passes
  - Related chapters: 1–20
  - Why it matters: it shows practical solutions to many of the exact same frontend problems

- **TinyCC (TCC)**
  - URL: https://bellard.org/tcc/tcc-doc.html
  - Authority tier: companion
  - Required or optional: optional
  - Consult when: you want a contrasting minimal implementation philosophy
  - Related chapters: 1–20
  - Why it matters: it shows what a tiny, pragmatic C compiler looks like, even if its tradeoffs differ from the book

- **LLVM project docs**
  - URL: https://llvm.org/docs/
  - Authority tier: companion
  - Required or optional: optional
  - Consult when: you want broader context for IR design, optimization, and backend infrastructure
  - Related chapters: 19, 20
  - Why it matters: LLVM is far larger than this project, but it provides good conceptual contrast
