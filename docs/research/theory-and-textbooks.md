# Theory and Textbooks

- **Engineering a Compiler (Cooper & Torczon)**
  - URL: https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/engineering-a-compiler/9780080916613/toc.xhtml
  - Authority tier: textbook
  - Required or optional: required
  - Consult when: designing parser architecture, data-flow analyses, or register allocation
  - Related chapters: 3, 5, 19, 20
  - Why it matters: it is one of the most practical advanced compiler texts for this project’s later chapters

- **Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools (Aho, Lam, Sethi, Ullman)**
  - URL: https://www.pearson.com/en-us/subject-catalog/p/compilers-principles-techniques-and-tools/P200000003472
  - Authority tier: textbook
  - Required or optional: recommended
  - Consult when: you want the canonical description of parsing, IRs, and optimization algorithms
  - Related chapters: 1–20
  - Why it matters: it is the standard cross-check source for compiler algorithms

- **Modern Compiler Implementation**
  - URL: https://www.cs.princeton.edu/~appel/modern/
  - Authority tier: textbook
  - Required or optional: recommended
  - Consult when: you want another perspective on IRs, activation records, and register allocation
  - Related chapters: 9, 10, 19, 20
  - Why it matters: Appel’s material is especially helpful for backend and regalloc concepts

- **Crafting Interpreters**
  - URL: https://craftinginterpreters.com/
  - Authority tier: high-value secondary
  - Required or optional: optional
  - Consult when: you want more intuition for recursive descent, AST structure, and incremental language implementation
  - Related chapters: 1–8
  - Why it matters: it is not a compiler-to-machine-code text, but it is excellent for parser and AST intuition
